How to Add a Favicon for WordPress: The 2026 Guide

If you’ve ever looked at your browser tabs and felt a pang of annoyance because your WordPress site still shows that generic grey globe or the default 'W' logo, you’re not alone. It’s the smallest detail that separates a 'hobby project' from a brand people actually trust. In 2026, users expect a seamless visual experience from the desktop tab to the mobile home screen bookmark.

The WordPress 'Site Icon' vs. Traditional Favicon

WordPress has its own way of doing things. They don't call it a favicon in the dashboard; they call it a Site Icon. The good news? WordPress handles a lot of the heavy lifting (like generating multiple sizes) automatically. The bad news? If you just upload a random low-res PNG, it’s going to look blurry on high-density displays like an iPhone or a MacBook Pro.

Before we start, you need a square image. I recommend a 512x512 pixel PNG or SVG. If your logo is a rectangle, don't just squash it. You need to create a simplified, square version. Look at Stripe: their main logo is a wordmark, but their favicon is a bold, simplified 'S' on a purple background. It’s legible even at 16x16 pixels. If you have a complex logo, use a tool like Mzu favicondl to test how it looks at smaller scales before uploading.

Method 1: The Modern Way (Block Themes & Site Editor)

If you are using a modern block theme (like Twenty Twenty-Four or Twenty Twenty-Five), the Customizer is often hidden. Here is how you find the setting in 2026:

  1. Navigate to Appearance > Editor from your WordPress dashboard.
  2. Click on the Styles icon (the half-filled circle in the top right).
  3. Click the Edit (pencil) icon to enter the style settings.
  4. Look for Site Identity (sometimes nested under 'General' or 'Header' depending on the theme).
  5. Click Select Site Icon and upload your 512x512 file.

Method 2: The Classic Way (Customizer)

For those still running classic themes or using page builders like Elementor, the old-school Customizer is your best friend. It’s faster and more direct.

  1. Go to Appearance > Customize.
  2. Find the Site Identity tab.
  3. Scroll down to the Site Icon section.
  4. Upload your image. WordPress will show you a preview of how it looks in a browser tab and as a mobile app icon.

If you find that your changes aren't appearing, it’s almost always a caching issue. Check out our guide on favicon not showing to troubleshoot the common culprits.

Method 3: The Developer Way (Manual Code)

Sometimes the built-in WordPress function doesn't give you enough control. Maybe you want to use a modern SVG favicon for better scaling and dark mode support. In that case, we skip the UI and go straight to the code.

Upload your favicon files (ICO, PNG, and SVG) to your theme folder (e.g., /wp-content/themes/your-theme/assets/favicons/). Then, add this to your functions.php file:

function mzu_custom_favicon() {
    echo '<link rel="icon" href="' . get_stylesheet_directory_uri() . '/assets/favicons/favicon.svg" type="image/svg+xml">';
    echo '<link rel="apple-touch-icon" href="' . get_stylesheet_directory_uri() . '/assets/favicons/apple-touch-icon.png">';
} 
add_action("wp_head", "mzu_custom_favicon");

This method ensures that your favicon is served exactly how you want it, without WordPress's automatic compression interfering with the quality.

Common Pitfalls to Avoid

I’ve audited hundreds of WordPress sites, and these are the three mistakes I see most often:

  • Ignoring the 512px rule: WordPress uses this large size to generate icons for mobile devices. If you upload a 16x16 file, your mobile 'Add to Home Screen' icon will look like a pixelated mess.
  • Forgetting Transparency: Unless your brand color is a solid square, use a transparent PNG or SVG. A white box around a circular logo looks amateurish in dark mode browser tabs.
  • Not Checking Dark Mode: If your logo is black, it will disappear in Chrome's dark mode. Consider adding a thin white stroke or using an SVG with media queries.

WordPress “站点图标”与传统 Favicon 的区别

WordPress 有一套自己的逻辑。在后台，它不叫 Favicon，而被称为站点图标 (Site Icon)。好消息是，WordPress 会自动帮你处理大部分繁重的工作（比如自动生成多种尺寸）。坏消息是，如果你只是随便上传一个低分辨率的 PNG，它在 iPhone 或 MacBook Pro 等高分屏上看起来会非常模糊。

在开始之前，你需要准备一张正方形图片。我建议使用 512x512 像素的 PNG 或 SVG。如果你的 Logo 是长方形的，千万不要直接挤压它。你需要设计一个简化的正方形版本。看看 Stripe：他们的主 Logo 是文字，但 Favicon 是一个紫色背景上大胆、简化的“S”。即使在 16x16 像素下也清晰可见。如果你不确定效果，可以使用 Mzu favicondl 这样的工具在上传前测试不同尺寸下的表现。

方法一：现代方式（区块主题与站点编辑器）

如果你使用的是现代区块主题（如 Twenty Twenty-Four），传统的“自定义”菜单可能被隐藏了。以下是 2026 年的操作步骤：

  1. 在 WordPress 仪表盘中进入 外观 > 编辑器。
  2. 点击右上角的 样式 图标（那个半圆符号）。
  3. 点击 编辑（铅笔图标）进入样式设置。
  4. 找到 站点身份 (Site Identity)（有时隐藏在“常规”或“页眉”选项下）。
  5. 点击 选择站点图标 并上传你的 512x512 文件。

方法二：经典方式（自定义器）

对于仍在使用经典主题或 Elementor 等页面构建器的用户，传统的“自定义器”是最快的方法。

  1. 进入 外观 > 自定义。
  2. 找到 站点身份 标签。
  3. 向下滚动到 站点图标 部分。
  4. 上传图片。WordPress 会为你展示在浏览器标签页和移动端应用图标上的预览效果。

如果你发现修改没生效，通常是缓存问题。可以参考我们的 favicon 不显示排查指南 来解决。

方法三：开发者方式（手动代码）

有时候，WordPress 自带的功能无法满足需求。比如你想使用现代的 SVG favicon 以获得更好的缩放效果和暗黑模式支持。这时我们可以跳过 UI，直接写代码。

将你的图标文件（ICO, PNG, SVG）上传到主题目录（例如 /wp-content/themes/your-theme/assets/favicons/），然后在 functions.php 中添加：

function mzu_custom_favicon() {
    echo '<link rel="icon" href="' . get_stylesheet_directory_uri() . '/assets/favicons/favicon.svg" type="image/svg+xml">';
    echo '<link rel="apple-touch-icon" href="' . get_stylesheet_directory_uri() . '/assets/favicons/apple-touch-icon.png">';
} 
add_action("wp_head", "mzu_custom_favicon");

这种方法能确保图标完全按照你的意图渲染，不受 WordPress 自动压缩的影响。

避坑指南

我审计过成百上千个 WordPress 网站，以下是三个最常见的错误：

  • 忽略 512px 规则： WordPress 需要大尺寸图片来生成移动端图标。如果你只上传 16x16 的文件，手机端的图标会模糊得像马赛克。
  • 忘记透明度： 除非你的品牌色本身就是实心方块，否则请使用透明的 PNG 或 SVG。在暗黑模式下，Logo 周围的一圈白框看起来非常不专业。
  • 不考虑暗黑模式： 如果你的 Logo 是纯黑色的，它在 Chrome 的暗黑模式标签页里会“消失”。考虑加个浅色描边或使用 SVG 媒体查询。
