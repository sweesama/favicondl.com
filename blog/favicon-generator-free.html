Pro Tips for a Perfect Favicon

Generating a favicon is one thing; generating a great favicon is another. Here are a few pro tips I've picked up over the years:

Start with a High-Resolution Source

This is non-negotiable. Don't upload a 32x32 pixel logo and expect miracles. Always start with your highest-resolution brand mark, ideally a vector graphic or a large PNG. Mzu favicondl can scale down beautifully, but it can't invent pixels.

Embrace Simplicity

Favicons are tiny. A complex, detailed logo will just become an unreadable blur. Think about brands like Google (just a 'G') or GitHub (the Octocat). Their favicons are instantly recognizable, even at 16x16 pixels. Simplify your design to its core essence for maximum impact.

Leverage SVG for Modernity

While ICO and PNG are still essential, SVG favicons are the future. They scale infinitely without pixelation and can even adapt to dark mode (a huge win for user experience!). Mzu favicondl generates SVG favicons, so make sure you include them in your HTML. (If you haven't explored them yet, our SVG Favicon Guide is a must-read).

Test, Test, Test

After deployment, always check your favicon on various browsers (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge) and devices (desktop, iOS, Android). Browser caching can be a nightmare, so clear your cache often during testing. (Having trouble? Our Favicon Not Showing? guide has common fixes).
