Perfect Favicon Size Squarespace Needs in 2026

You just uploaded your gorgeous new logo to Squarespace, refreshed your browser, and the tab icon looks like a blurry, pixelated smudge. If you've ever wondered why your site looks professional on desktop but shows a low-res mess in Safari's tab bar, your source image dimensions are likely fighting with the platform's aggressive image processor.

Getting the perfect favicon size squarespace demands isn't about guessing. It requires specific dimensions to survive their content delivery network (CDN) without losing edge sharpness.

The Ideal Favicon Size Squarespace Needs in 2026

Squarespace technically accepts anything from 100x100 to 300x300 pixels. I strongly recommend ignoring the lower end and uploading exactly 300x300 pixels. Why? Retina displays.

Look at Stripe or GitHub. They don't upload 16x16 legacy files anymore. They use crisp, high-resolution source images that scale down gracefully. When you upload a 300x300 PNG, Squarespace's CDN automatically generates the smaller sizes needed for different devices without destroying the pixels.

Prerequisites for a Crisp Tab Icon

Before you touch the upload button, verify your image meets these strict baselines:

  • Format: PNG (never JPEG, you need transparency).
  • Aspect Ratio: 1:1 (a perfect square).
  • File Size: Strictly under 100KB.

Step-by-Step: Sizing and Uploading

Here is the exact developer workflow to ensure your icon stays sharp across all browsers.

  1. Open your design tool and create a 300x300 pixel canvas.
  2. Center your logo, leaving a 10% transparent margin around the edges (the safe zone).
  3. Export as a standard PNG with transparency enabled.
  4. Compress the file using Mzu favicondl to strip unnecessary metadata before uploading.

How Squarespace Processes Your File

If you inspect your published Squarespace site using Chrome DevTools, you will see how they rewrite your HTML:

<link rel='icon' href='https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/.../favicon.png?format=100w'>

Notice that ?format=100w parameter. Squarespace dynamically serves a 100-pixel wide version to modern browsers. If your original upload was only 32x32, their CDN attempts to upscale it, resulting in that dreaded blurriness.

Common Pitfalls to Avoid

Uploading non-square images is the most common mistake. If you upload a rectangular logo, Squarespace will forcefully crop or squish it. Always use a 1:1 canvas.

Another trap is ignoring dark mode. If your logo is solid black with a transparent background, it will completely vanish for users with dark browser themes. Use a white outline or a solid background color. For more details on this, check our guide on transparent backgrounds.
