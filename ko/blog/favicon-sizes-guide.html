Favicon Sizes Guide: What Sizes Do You Really Need?

With so many devices, browsers, and platforms, favicon sizes can be confusing. Do you need 6 different files? Or can you get away with just one? This guide cuts through the noise.

The Minimal Setup (3 Files)

If you want to keep things simple, these 3 files cover 99% of real-world scenarios:

  1. favicon.ico (32×32) — The universal fallback. Every browser supports it.
  2. apple-touch-icon.png (180×180) — For iOS devices and some Android launchers.
  3. site.webmanifest — Points to your 192×192 and 512×512 icons for PWA support.
<link rel="icon" href="/favicon.ico" sizes="32x32">
<link rel="apple-touch-icon" href="/apple-touch-icon.png">
<link rel="manifest" href="/site.webmanifest">

Tip: Modern browsers actually prefer PNG over ICO. If you include a 32×32 PNG alongside the ICO, modern browsers will use the PNG for crisper rendering.

Why Not Just Use One Large Image?

A common mistake is providing only a 512×512 icon and expecting browsers to scale it down. Here's why that's problematic:

  • Performance — A 512×512 PNG can be 50KB+. Browser tabs only need 16×16 (under 1KB). That's 50x more data than necessary.
  • Quality — Downscaling a complex logo from 512px to 16px often produces a blurry mess. Hand-optimized small icons look much better.
  • ICO compatibility — Older browsers and Windows shortcuts still expect ICO format.

为什么不能只用一张大图？

一个常见的误区是只提供 512×512 的图标，期望浏览器自动缩小。为什么这样不好：

  • 性能 — 512×512 的 PNG 可能超过 50KB。浏览器标签页只需要 16×16（不到 1KB），多了 50 倍的数据。
  • 质量 — 把复杂的 Logo 从 512px 缩小到 16px 通常会变成模糊的一团。手动优化的小图标看起来好得多。
  • ICO 兼容性 — 旧版浏览器和 Windows 快捷方式仍然需要 ICO 格式。
