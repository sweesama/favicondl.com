Favicon for SaaS Landing Pages: Stop Losing Tab Hoarders

If you've ever watched a potential customer evaluate your software against three competitors, you know they hoard browser tabs. They open your pricing page, a competitor's feature list, and maybe a Reddit review thread. If your site shows a generic grey globe while the others display crisp, recognizable logos, you've already lost a tiny fraction of trust before they even click back.

A favicon for SaaS landing pages isn't just a branding exercise. It is a persistent UI anchor. When a user navigates away to check their email, that 16x16 pixel square is the only visual cue calling them back to finish signing up.

The Tab Hoarder Problem in SaaS

SaaS buyers evaluate software based on polish. We assume that if a company can't be bothered to fix a blurry browser icon, their actual application is probably full of bugs too. It sounds harsh, but browser tabs are a highly competitive real estate.

Look at Vercel. Their black triangle on a white background (which seamlessly inverts for dark mode) is unmistakable. Even if you have 40 tabs open and the text labels disappear completely, you can spot the Vercel dashboard instantly. That is deliberate tab-bar dominance.

Your SaaS landing page needs to achieve that same level of instant recognition.
